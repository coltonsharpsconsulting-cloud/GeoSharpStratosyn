# ASA & AAS Proofs

Lesson 24 gave you SSS and SAS — force a triangle with three sides, or two sides and the angle between them. This week flips it: two **angles** and just **one** side. Because a triangle's angles always sum to 180°, knowing two angles already fixes the whole shape; one real length then fixes the size. ASA uses the side *between* the two given angles; AAS uses a side that isn't. Either way, exactly one triangle fits.

## Words to own
- **ASA Postulate** — two angles and the **included** side (the side that is a ray of both angles).
- **AAS Postulate** — two angles and a **non-included** side (adjacent to only one of the two given angles).
- **Included side** vs. **non-included side** — the whole difference between the two postulates.
- **Reflexive Property** — a side or angle shared by both triangles, congruent to itself, no extra work needed.
- **CPCTC** — once two triangles are proven congruent, every other corresponding pair of parts is congruent too.
