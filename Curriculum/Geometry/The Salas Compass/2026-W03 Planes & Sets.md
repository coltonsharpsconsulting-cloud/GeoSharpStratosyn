# Planes & Sets

Put your hand flat on the table. That tabletop — if it kept going forever in every direction, never curving, never stopping — is a **plane**. Everything you've drawn so far, every point and line and segment, has secretly been living on one plane the whole time.

Now open a kitchen drawer. Some of what's in there is silverware. Some of it is round. A few things are both. That drawer is a **set**, and the moment you start sorting "silverware" from "round things" from "both," you're doing exactly what this week is about.

## Words to own
- **Plane** — flat, two-dimensional, forever in every direction. Named with one letter (plane M) or three points not on a line (plane ABC).
- **Coplanar** — points or lines sharing the same plane.
- **Set** — a collection of things. **Empty/null set** ($\varnothing$) — a set with nothing in it.
- **Union** ($\cup$) — everything in either set, combined.
- **Intersection** ($\cap$) — only what's shared by both.
- **Subset** ($\subset$) — one group entirely contained in another.
- Two facts to keep cold: two lines cross at **one point**; two planes meet along **one line**.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Find **two groups of things** in your house that overlap a little — like "things that are round" and "things made of metal." Name three items that belong to *both* groups (the intersection) and one item that belongs to only one. Sketch it in your Compass.
