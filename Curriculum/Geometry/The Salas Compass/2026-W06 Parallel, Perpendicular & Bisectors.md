# Parallel & Perpendicular Lines; Bisectors

Look at the two rails of a set of shelves. They run side by side, same distance apart, forever — that's **parallel**. Now look at where a wall meets the floor. They cross once, and the corner they make is a perfect square corner — that's **perpendicular**. Every pair of straight lines you'll ever draw is one of these two, or neither.

Now fold a piece of paper exactly in half. The crease cuts the paper's edge into two equal pieces — that crease is a **bisector**. If it also crosses that edge at a perfect right angle, it's earned a fancier name: a **perpendicular bisector**. And the exact point where it crosses? That's the **midpoint**.

## Words to own
- **Parallel** ($\parallel$) — same plane, never intersect. **Perpendicular** ($\perp$) — intersect, forming a right angle.
- **Bisector** — cuts an angle or a segment into two congruent parts. *(Not every bisector of a segment is perpendicular to it — only one special one is.)*
- **Perpendicular bisector** — a bisector that also crosses at exactly 90°.
- **Midpoint** — the one point that splits a segment into two congruent halves.
- **Straightedge + compass** — the two tools that find a midpoint or bisect an angle exactly, with no ruler and no protractor.
