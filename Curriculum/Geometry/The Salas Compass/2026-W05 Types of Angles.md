# Types of Angles

Picture a door swinging open from fully closed. At first the gap between the door and the frame is tiny — an **acute** angle. Keep pushing, and the moment the door stands squarely away from the wall, you've hit a **right** angle — 90°, an exact square corner. Push a little further and the gap widens into an **obtuse** angle — bigger than square, but not yet flat. Let the door swing all the way until it lies flat against an adjoining wall, and its two edges make one straight line — a **straight** angle, 180°. Now imagine a hinge strong enough to keep going past that point — the angle you'd measure the long way around, past flat, is a **reflex** angle.

Same door, same hinge, five different names — and the only thing that changed is the number of degrees.

## Words to own
- **Acute** — greater than 0° and less than 90°.
- **Right** — exactly 90°. Marked with a small square, not an arc.
- **Obtuse** — greater than 90° and less than 180°.
- **Straight** — exactly 180°. Two opposite rays, one unbroken line.
- **Reflex** — greater than 180° and less than 360°. The "far side" of any smaller angle.
- **The rule:** add angle measures like plain numbers, then reclassify the total — right + acute = obtuse; right + right = straight; straight + acute = reflex.
