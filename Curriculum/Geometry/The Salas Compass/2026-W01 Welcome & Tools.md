# Welcome to Geometry

Geometry means *"measure of the earth."* For thousands of years, people used a stretched rope and a straight stick to lay out fields, temples, and pyramids — and got them square to within inches. You're about to learn the same moves, with better tools.

Here's the deal for our year: **three days a week we meet** — one day to learn an idea (**Concept**), one day to build it with your hands or in CAD (**Build**), one day to reason it out and check it (**Prove**). Between our sessions, the Math-U-See work is yours to do. I'll make a lot; you'll build a lot; together we'll make it stick.

This notebook — **The Salas Compass** — is where it all lives: your reads, your sketches, your build photos, your goals. Start it today.

## Words to own
- **Compass** — draws circles and steps off equal lengths. *The width of the compass is the length.*
- **Straightedge** — draws a straight line. (Not a ruler — during a construction we don't measure with it.)
- **Protractor** — measures angles in degrees.

## Before Session 1
- Have your tools out: compass, straightedge, protractor, a sharp pencil.
- On the first page of this notebook, write today's date and **one goal** for the year — something you want to be able to *build* by spring.

*One dot on a page can mark an exact spot in a world of infinitely many places. That's where we begin next week.*
