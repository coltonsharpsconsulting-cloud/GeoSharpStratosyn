# Transformations

A video game sprite doesn't get bigger or smaller when it slides across the screen — it just moves. This week names that "just moves" idea precisely: a **translation** slides a figure, a **reflection** flips it over a line like a mirror, and a **rotation** turns it around a fixed point. All three keep the figure exactly the same size and shape — only its position (and sometimes which way it's facing) changes.

Each move has its own coordinate rule. Translate by adding a fixed amount to every x and every y. Reflect over the x-axis by flipping the sign of y; reflect over the y-axis by flipping the sign of x. Rotate 90° about the origin by swapping x and y (and flipping one sign); rotate 180° by flipping both signs. Every rule does the identical thing to every point of the figure — that sameness is exactly why the image is congruent to the original.

## Words to own
- **Translation** — a slide: (x, y) → (x + h, y + k).
- **Reflection** — a flip over a line; over the x-axis: (x, y) → (x, −y); over the y-axis: (x, y) → (−x, y).
- **Rotation** — a turn about a fixed point; 90° about the origin: (x, y) → (−y, x); 180°: (x, y) → (−x, −y).
- **Isometry (rigid motion)** — a transformation that preserves every length and every angle, so the image is always congruent to the pre-image.
