# Special Right Triangles: 30-60-90

Slice an equilateral triangle exactly in half and a second special right triangle falls out — one whose three sides always lock into the same ratio, no matter how big or small the triangle is: $1 : \sqrt{3} : 2$.

The **short leg** sits opposite the 30° angle and is always the shortest side. The **long leg**, opposite 60°, is always the short leg times $\sqrt{3}$. The **hypotenuse**, opposite the right angle, is always exactly twice the short leg. Know just one side, and the other two follow immediately.

## Words to own
- **Short leg** — opposite 30°, the shortest side. **Long leg** — opposite 60°, equal to short leg $\times \sqrt{3}$. **Hypotenuse** — opposite 90°, equal to short leg $\times 2$.
- **The ratio** — $\text{short} : \text{long} : \text{hypotenuse} = 1 : \sqrt{3} : 2$. Keep radical answers in **simplest form** (Lessons 17–19's skill comes right back).
- **Working backward from the hypotenuse** — divide by 2 to get the short leg, then multiply that answer by $\sqrt{3}$ to get the long leg.
