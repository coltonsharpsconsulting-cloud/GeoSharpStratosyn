# Polygons, Quadrilaterals & Perimeter

Look at a picture frame. Four straight sides, closed up into one figure — that's a **quadrilateral**. Now check its corners with a square edge: if all four are right angles, it's a **rectangle**; if the sides are also all equal, it's actually a **square** — a rectangle that happens to qualify twice. Walk your finger all the way around the frame's edge, adding as you go, and you've just found its **perimeter**.

You've spent weeks pinning down points, planes, and angles one at a time. This week those pieces click together into whole closed shapes — and you'll see that "square," "rectangle," "rhombus," and "trapezoid" aren't six unrelated words to memorize, but one family tree with a few simple extra rules stacked on top of each other.

## Words to own
- **Polygon** — any closed figure made of straight sides. **Quadrilateral** — any four-sided polygon.
- **Parallelogram** — two pairs of parallel sides. **Rectangle** — a parallelogram with four right angles. **Rhombus** — a parallelogram with four congruent sides.
- **Square** — four congruent sides AND four right angles (a rectangle that's also a rhombus).
- **Trapezoid** — only ONE pair of parallel sides — never also a parallelogram.
- **Perimeter** — the distance around a figure; add every side, including the "steps" in an L-shaped figure.
- Interior angles: a **triangle** always sums to **180°**; a **quadrilateral** always sums to **360°**.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Find **one quadrilateral-shaped object** in your house (a window, a rug, a tile, a laptop lid) and measure all four sides. Classify it — square? rectangle? parallelogram? something else? — and add the four sides to find its perimeter. Sketch it in your Compass and label the sides.
