# Unit 03 Studio

Unit 03 is where geometry stopped asking you to measure and started asking you to prove. Special right triangles gave you exact ratios instead of a calculator's approximation. Congruence and similarity gave you the language — SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, HL, AA — to say precisely why two triangles must match, and two-column proofs made you write the reasoning down, statement by statement. Transformations moved figures around the coordinate plane, and trigonometry finally connected an angle to a side you couldn't reach with a ruler.

Session 1 is a fast lap back through Lessons 20–30 — special triangles, proof, similarity, transformations, and the six trig ratios — redrawing the map so the gaps show before they cost you on the test. Session 2 is the capstone: measure something real with an angle and trig, then transform a figure on the coordinate plane and check that it stayed rigid. Session 3 is the real test — a final mixed review, then Math-U-See Unit Test III, on your own, no notes.

## Words to own (a sampler from the unit)
- **CPCTC** — once triangles are proven congruent, every corresponding side and angle is congruent too.
- **AA similarity** — two congruent angle pairs are enough to prove two triangles similar (same shape, proportional sides).
- **SOH-CAH-TOA** — sine = opposite/hypotenuse, cosine = adjacent/hypotenuse, tangent = opposite/adjacent.

## Before Session 2 (bring an answer)
Scout a real object you can measure by angle — a tree, a roofline, a flagpole, a distance across the yard — and think about where you'd stand and what you'd measure with a protractor or an inclinometer app. Separately, sketch a simple polygon on graph paper that you'll translate, reflect, or rotate. Bring both plans so Build Day is spent measuring and proving, not still deciding what to do.

> This is a studio week, not a new lesson — the goal is to leave with Unit 03 solid enough to build on, and a passed MUS Unit Test III in hand.
