# Year-End Final

This is it. One last mixed review and a materials check, then the MUS Final Exam — cumulative, independent, no help while it's in progress — then a debrief and a real look back at the whole year.

Same rule that's held all year for every Lesson Test and Unit Test: if you get stuck mid-exam, you skip it and flag it. You don't look it up, and you don't ask. That's not a punishment — it's what makes the score actually mean something.

## Words to own
- **Independent assessment** — completed alone, no notes, no help, no AI tool, while it's in progress.
- **Flag it** — skip a stuck problem and come back to debrief it afterward, instead of guessing or looking it up.

## Before Session 1
Get your Test Booklet, pencil, ruler, and protractor together now, before the session — not the morning of. Then think back to Week 1: what's one thing about geometry you understand now that you didn't then?

> Before the exam, reread **§7 Assessment Integrity** in the [[Curriculum/Geometry/Class Docs/Geometry — Course Manual|Course Manual]] — the Final Exam is completed independently, same as every Lesson Test all year.
