# Angle Pairs

Open a pair of scissors halfway. The two blades meet at the pivot — that's the vertex — and the angle between them sits right next to the angle on the *other* side of one blade, sharing that blade like a common wall. Those two angles are **adjacent**. Now picture a crossroads seen from above: two straight roads crossing at one point make **four** angles, and the two facing each other across the intersection — say, northeast and southwest — will always measure exactly the same, whether or not the road crew ever checked.

This week you stop looking at one angle alone and start looking at *pairs* — and you'll meet one pairing that's more than just usually true. It's guaranteed.

## Words to own
- **Adjacent angles** — share a vertex and a side, don't overlap.
- **Vertical angles** — the pair directly opposite each other where two lines cross. **Always congruent.**
- **Complementary angles** — two measures that add to **90°**.
- **Supplementary angles** — two measures that add to **180°**.
- **Linear pair** — adjacent angles whose outer sides form a straight line; always supplementary.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Find one real **X** in your house — scissors half-open, a folded napkin corner, two shoelaces crossed, a window's crossed mullions. Sketch it in your Compass, number the four angles it makes, and circle the pair you think will measure the same without you checking.
