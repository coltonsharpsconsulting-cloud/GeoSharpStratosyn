# The Pythagorean Theorem

Look at any ramp, ladder, roof pitch, or squared foundation corner: somewhere behind it sits the same short equation, over two thousand years old and still exactly true — $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

A right triangle has one 90° angle. The two sides that form it are the **legs**; the side across from it, always the longest, is the **hypotenuse**. Square each leg, add them, and you get the hypotenuse squared — and it runs backward too: give the theorem three side lengths, and its **converse** tells you whether they make a right triangle at all, no protractor required.

## Words to own
- **Legs** ($a$, $b$) — the two sides that meet at the right angle. **Hypotenuse** ($c$) — the longest side, opposite the right angle.
- **Pythagorean theorem** — $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$. Solve for a missing hypotenuse or leg, and keep radical answers in **simplest form** (Lesson 17's skill comes right back).
- **Converse** — if $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ is true for three given lengths (with $c$ the longest), the triangle they form must be right.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Mark a string at 3, 4, and 5 equal units and pull it taut into a triangle — guess, before you check with a protractor, whether the corner between the 3 and 4 sides will be exactly 90°.
