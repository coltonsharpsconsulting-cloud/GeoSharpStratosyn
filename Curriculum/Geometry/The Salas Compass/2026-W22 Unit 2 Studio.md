# Unit 02 Studio

Unit 02 took you from flat polygons into three dimensions — and brought a new toolkit of numbers with it. You measured circles by their radius and π, filled solids with volume and wrapped them in surface area, then picked up the algebra of radicals so you could work with the square roots the Pythagorean theorem keeps handing you. Nine lessons, and every one of them is really about the same question: given a shape, how much of it is there?

Session 1 is a fast lap back through Lessons 11–19 — circles, solids, radicals, right triangles — redrawing the map so the gaps show before they cost you on the test. Session 2 is the capstone: build or 3D-print a real solid, then prove with your own ruler that its volume and surface area match what the formulas predict. Session 3 is the real test — a final mixed review, then Math-U-See Unit Test II, on your own, no notes.

## Words to own (a sampler from the unit)
- **Volume** — how much space a solid holds; **surface area** — the total area of every face wrapped around it.
- **Radical** — a square root expression; simplify by pulling out the largest perfect-square factor.
- **Pythagorean theorem** — a² + b² = c², the relationship every right triangle obeys.

## Before Session 2 (bring an answer)
Decide what solid you'll build or print — a box, a cylinder, a cone, a pyramid, a prism. Sketch it with its dimensions labeled (or rough out what you'll set in CAD) so Build Day is spent constructing and measuring, not still deciding what to make.

> This is a studio week, not a new lesson — the goal is to leave with Unit 02 solid enough to build on, and a passed MUS Unit Test II in hand.
