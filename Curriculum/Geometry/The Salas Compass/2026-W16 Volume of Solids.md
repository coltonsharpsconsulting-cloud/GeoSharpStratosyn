# Volume of Rectangular Solids & Cylinders

Look around: a cereal box, a soup can, a shipping box, the room you're sitting in. Each one holds an exact amount of space — and that amount has an exact number, found the same way every time: find the area of the **base**, then multiply by the **height**.

A rectangle's area was base times height, flat. A rectangular solid's volume is the same idea with a rectangle for a base — length times width times height. A cylinder swaps in a circle for a base — πr² times height. Same rule, different base.

## Words to own
- **Volume** — the amount of space a solid fills, measured in **cubic units** (in³, ft³, cm³) — never square or linear units.
- **Rectangular solid** — $V = l \cdot w \cdot h$. **Cylinder** — $V = \pi r^2 \cdot h$, where the base's area (πr²) gets found first, then multiplied by the height.
- **Face, edge, vertex** — a rectangular solid's flat surfaces, the lines where two faces meet, and the points where edges meet (6 faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices).

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Find **one container at home** that's a rectangular solid or a cylinder (a box, a can, a candle, a mug) — sketch it in your Compass, mark every dimension you'd need to find its volume, and guess the volume before you measure and compute it.
