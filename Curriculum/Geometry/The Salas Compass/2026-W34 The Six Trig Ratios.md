# The Six Trigonometric Ratios

You already own SOHCAHTOA — sin, cos, tan. This week they get their three missing partners, and the trick is almost embarrassingly simple: **flip the fraction upside down.** sin θ = opp/hyp becomes csc θ = hyp/opp. Flip cos and you get sec. Flip tan and you get cot. Six ratios, three pairs, one move.

## Words to own
- **Cosecant (csc θ)** — the reciprocal of sine: hyp/opp.
- **Secant (sec θ)** — the reciprocal of cosine: hyp/adj.
- **Cotangent (cot θ)** — the reciprocal of tangent: adj/opp.
- **Reciprocal** — flip the fraction; multiply a ratio by its reciprocal and you always get 1.
- **sin²θ + cos²θ = 1** — the Pythagorean theorem, expressed with trig ratios. True for every right triangle.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
A right triangle has legs 6 and 8 and a hypotenuse of 10. Without looking anything up, write sin θ and its reciprocal csc θ (reduced), for θ opposite the leg of 6. Then multiply the two — what do you get? Write your reasoning in your Compass.
