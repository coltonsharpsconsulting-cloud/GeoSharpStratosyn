# Sine, Cosine & Tangent

Pick one acute angle θ in a right triangle and every other side gets a name relative to it: the **hypotenuse** (opposite the right angle, always the longest side), the **opposite side** (across from θ), and the **adjacent side** (touching θ). Three fixed ratios follow — **SOH-CAH-TOA**: $\sin\theta = \dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}$, $\cos\theta = \dfrac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}}$, $\tan\theta = \dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}$.

Given all three sides, these are just reduced fractions. Given one side and the angle, set the ratio up as an equation and solve with a calculator's sin/cos/tan key (degree mode) — that's how you find a height you can't reach with a ruler.

## Words to own
- **Opposite / adjacent side** — named relative to a chosen acute angle θ; they trade places if you pick the other acute angle instead.
- **Hypotenuse** — the side opposite the right angle; it never changes no matter which acute angle you're using.
- **SOH-CAH-TOA** — sin θ = opp/hyp, cos θ = adj/hyp, tan θ = opp/adj.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
A 20-foot ladder leans against a wall at a 62° angle with the ground. Without touching a ruler to the wall, guess: about how high up does the ladder reach — and which of sin, cos, or tan would you use to check it exactly?
