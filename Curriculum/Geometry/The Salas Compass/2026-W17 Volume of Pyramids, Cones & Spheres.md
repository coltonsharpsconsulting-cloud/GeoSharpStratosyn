# Volume of Pyramids, Cones & Spheres

Last week every solid stood up straight, so volume was just base times height. This week two of your solids — a pyramid and a cone — taper the whole way up to a single point, called the **apex**. That taper changes exactly one thing: multiply base area times height like always, then multiply by **one third**. A sphere skips the base-times-height idea entirely and gets its own formula, built only from its radius.

## Words to own
- **Apex** — the single point where a pyramid's faces (or a cone's surface) meet. **Altitude** — the straight-down, perpendicular height that belongs in the volume formula. **Slant height** — the height of a tilted *face*, measured along the surface — never used for volume.
- **Prism** (general case of last week's box) — two congruent, parallel bases, straight parallelogram sides: $V = B \cdot h$, no ⅓.
- **Pyramid** — $V = \tfrac{1}{3}Bh$. **Cone** — $V = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$. Both exactly ⅓ of the prism/cylinder sharing their base and height.
- **Sphere** — $V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$ — no base, no height, no exception. Round sphere answers to the nearest hundredth.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Find **one cone- or pyramid-shaped object at home** (an ice-cream cone, a party hat, a paperweight) — sketch it in your Compass, mark its apex and its altitude, and guess whether its volume is closer to ⅓, ½, or the full volume of a box or cylinder sharing its base and height.
