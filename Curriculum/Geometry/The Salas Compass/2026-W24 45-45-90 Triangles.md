# Special Right Triangles: 45-45-90

Cut a square exactly along its diagonal and you get two identical right triangles: a 90° corner and two 45° corners. Because those two 45° angles are equal, the two legs facing them must be equal too — an **isosceles right triangle**. That one fact collapses the Pythagorean theorem into a single shortcut: $\text{hypotenuse} = \text{leg} \times \sqrt{2}$.

Every 45-45-90 triangle, no matter its size, keeps the same **ratio**: leg : leg : hypotenuse = $1 : 1 : \sqrt{2}$. Know one leg and you instantly know all three sides — no separate squaring, adding, and rooting. Run it backward and you're dividing by $\sqrt{2}$, which means rationalizing the denominator — Lesson 19's skill, right on cue.

## Words to own
- **45-45-90 triangle / isosceles right triangle** — a right triangle with two 45° angles and two congruent legs.
- **The rule** — $\text{hyp} = \text{leg}\sqrt{2}$; backward, $\text{leg} = \dfrac{\text{hyp}}{\sqrt{2}} = \dfrac{\text{hyp}\sqrt{2}}{2}$ (rationalized).
- **Ratio** — leg : leg : hypotenuse = $1:1:\sqrt{2}$, constant for every 45-45-90 triangle.
