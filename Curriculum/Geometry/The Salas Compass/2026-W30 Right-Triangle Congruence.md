# Right-Triangle Congruence

Every right triangle hands you one congruent angle for free — the right angle itself. This week asks what that freebie buys you: instead of needing three matching parts to prove two triangles congruent, a right triangle only needs **two** more. Four named shortcuts — HL, LL, HA, LA — are really SSS, SAS, AAS, and ASA wearing right-triangle clothes, and HL is the one that gets its "extra" side from the Pythagorean theorem instead of from a measurement.

## Words to own
- **HL (Hypotenuse-Leg)** — hypotenuse ≅ hypotenuse, one leg ≅ one leg; really SSS, since the Pythagorean theorem forces the third leg to match too.
- **LL (Leg-Leg)** — both legs ≅ both legs; really SAS, since the right angle always sits included between them.
- **HA (Hypotenuse-Angle)** — hypotenuse ≅ hypotenuse, one acute angle ≅ one acute angle; really AAS.
- **LA (Leg-Angle)** — one leg ≅ one leg, one acute angle ≅ one acute angle; really ASA or AAS, depending on where the leg sits.
- **SSA** — two sides and a non-included angle; NOT a valid shortcut in general. HL is the lone exception.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Before Session 1, try this yourself: two right triangles both have a hypotenuse of 10 and one leg of 6. Without drawing a single extra measurement, use the Pythagorean theorem to find the other leg for BOTH triangles. Are the two triangles forced to be identical? Write your reasoning in your Compass — even a rough version. You'll compare it to the formal version in Session 1.
