# Unit 01 Studio

Ten lessons, one map. Since Week 2 you've built the whole vocabulary of flat geometry from scratch — points and lines, angles and their pairs, parallels cut by a transversal, polygons and the formulas that measure them, and triangles sorted two ways at once. None of that was separate. A parallelogram's area only makes sense because you know what a base and a height are; a triangle only closes if its sides obey the inequality you proved in Lesson 10. This week is where all ten pieces get to sit at the same table.

Session 1 is a fast lap back through Lessons 1–10 — not new material, just the map redrawn so the gaps show. Session 2 is the capstone: you choose a polygon and build it yourself, by compass-and-straightedge or CAD, then prove with your own ruler and protractor that its perimeter and interior angle sum are exactly what geometry says they should be. Session 3 is the real test — a final mixed review, then Math-U-See Unit Test I, on your own, no notes.

## Words to own (a sampler from the unit)
- **Perimeter** — the sum of a polygon's side lengths. **Interior angle sum** — (n − 2)·180°, no matter how the polygon is shaped.
- **Supplementary / complementary / vertical angles** — 180°, 90°, and the always-equal pair across an X.
- **Triangle inequality** — any two sides must add to MORE than the third, or the triangle can't close.

## Before Session 2 (bring an answer)
Pick the polygon you want to build — a triangle, a quadrilateral, or a pentagon. If it's a triangle, check your three chosen side lengths against the triangle inequality BEFORE you build, the way you did with real sticks back in Week 11. Sketch your planned polygon, with every vertex labeled, so you can walk into the build session ready to construct instead of still deciding.

> This is a studio week, not a new lesson — the goal is to leave with Unit 01 solid enough to build on, and a passed MUS Unit Test I in hand.
