# Angles

Open a laptop screen halfway. That hinge is a **vertex** — the exact point where two straight edges meet and lean apart. The screen and the base are two **rays** sharing that one point, and the amount they've swung apart from each other has an exact number: a **degree** measure. Close the laptop flat and the same hinge now holds a **straight angle**, 180° — the screen and base form one unbroken line.

You've spent two weeks pinning down points, lines, and planes. This week you pick up the tool that measures the *turn* between two rays — the protractor — and you'll use it both ways: to read an angle that's already there, and to draw one to an exact spec.

## Words to own
- **Angle** — two rays sharing a common endpoint. **Vertex** — that shared endpoint. **Sides** — the two rays.
- **∠ABC** — three-letter name, vertex letter always in the middle. **∠B** — one-letter name, only when there's no ambiguity. **∠1** — a numbered label in a diagram.
- **m∠ABC** — read "the measure of angle ABC" — degrees, 0° to 360°.
- **Protractor** — reads 0° to 180°; watch for its two scales and use the one that starts at 0° on your baseline ray.
- **Straight angle** — two opposite rays forming one line, exactly 180°. A quarter of that turn is a **right angle**, exactly 90°.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Find **one real hinge** in your house — a door, a laptop, a cabinet, a pair of scissors — and estimate its angle in degrees just by eye. Then check yourself with a protractor if you have one. Sketch it in your Compass and label the vertex.
