# Congruent Triangles

Last week you learned to sort every angle fact you own into "assumed" (postulates) and "proven" (theorems). This week hands you the shape those proofs are actually about: two triangles, and the exact promise their congruence makes. △ABC ≅ △DEF isn't a vague "these look alike" — it's six separate matches (three sides, three angles) named, in order, by the letters themselves. Get the order wrong and you've named a different correspondence entirely, even if the picture never moved.

## Words to own
- **Congruent triangles** — all three corresponding sides AND all three corresponding angles are congruent. Six matches, not one impression.
- **Correspondence** — which vertex, side, and angle of one triangle pairs with which of the other, decided by the ORDER of letters in the name, not by position on the page.
- **Congruence notation** — △ABC ≅ △DEF: 1st letter with 1st, 2nd with 2nd, 3rd with 3rd. Swap two letters and you've named a different pairing.
- **CPCTC** — Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles are Congruent. Once two triangles are known congruent, every one of the six pairs is congruent, even ones nobody measured.

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Before Session 1, try this yourself: draw two identical triangles, laid out the same way on the page. Write a congruence statement for them where the letters are in the "obvious" left-to-right order — then write a SECOND statement for the exact same two triangles with just two letters swapped. Do both statements describe the same correspondence? Sketch both and note which points actually pair up in each case. Write your answer in your Compass — even a rough version. You'll compare it to the formal version in Session 1.
