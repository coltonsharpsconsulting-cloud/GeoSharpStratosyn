# Circles

For the last several weeks you've built shapes out of straight sides — polygons, their diagonals, their interior angles. This week the straight sides disappear. A circle has no corners at all, just one **center** and every other point curving away from it at the exact same distance — that distance is the **radius**.

A chord that runs straight through the center is a **diameter**, always exactly twice the radius. A chord that doesn't is just a **chord**. A line that grazes the circle at one point is a **tangent**; a line that cuts through at two points is a **secant**. A piece of the rim is an **arc**; a piece of the pie is a **sector** — and half the circle, exactly 180° of arc, is a **semicircle**.

## Words to own
- **Center** — the one point equidistant from the whole circle. **Radius** — center to rim; every radius of one circle is congruent.
- **Diameter** — a chord through the center, $d = 2r$, and the longest chord there is.
- **Chord** — a segment with both ends on the circle. **Secant** — the same idea as a full line, two points. **Tangent** — a line touching just one point, perpendicular to the radius there.
- **Arc** — a piece of the circumference; minor + major arc always total 360°. **Semicircle** — an arc of exactly 180°.
- **Sector** — a pie-slice piece of the circle's area, boxed in by two radii and an arc.
- Bonus: a **sphere** is a three-dimensional circle; an **ellipse** is a stretched circle (an orbit, an oval, a race track).

## Build-log prompt (bring an answer)
Find **one real circle** in your house — a plate, a clock face, a wheel, a lid — and estimate its radius and diameter just by eye, then check yourself with a ruler across the middle. Sketch it in your Compass and label the center, the radius, and the diameter.
