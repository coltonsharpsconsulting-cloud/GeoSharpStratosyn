# Points, Lines, Rays & Segments

Look around the room right now. The edge of your desk is a **segment** — it starts and stops. A beam of sunlight through the window is a **ray** — it starts at the sun and just… keeps going. The horizon is our best try at a **line**. And the exact corner where two walls and the floor meet? That's a **point** — no size, pure location.

All of geometry — every triangle, circle, and solid you'll build this year — is made of these four things. They differ by one tiny count: **how many ends stop.**

## Words to own
- **Point** — a location, no size. Named with a capital letter.
- **Line** — straight, forever both ways. *(arrows both ends)*
- **Ray** — one endpoint, forever one way. *(write the endpoint first!)*
- **Segment** — two endpoints, measurable. *(the workhorse of building)*
- **Collinear** — points on the same line. **Congruent** — same shape and size.
