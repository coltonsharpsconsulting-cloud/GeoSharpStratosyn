# Fold & Measure — 45-45-90 in Real Life

*Every square you fold along its diagonal makes the exact same triangle: two 45° corners, one 90° corner, and a hypotenuse that's always leg × √2. Today you build one with your own hands, measure it, and then go find the same triangle somewhere else in the real world.*

**Time:** ~1 hour · **Deliverable:** a photographed square-fold 45-45-90 triangle with its sides measured, plus a second measured real-world 45-45-90 object, for your [[Records/Portfolio|Portfolio]].

## Materials
- A square piece of paper (or a square tile, napkin, or cut cardboard) — the more exactly square, the better
- A ruler or tape measure, marked in a consistent unit (inches or cm)
- A 45-45-90 set-square (drafting triangle), if you have one — or any object with a diagonally-cut square corner (a mitered picture-frame corner, a cut floor tile)
- A calculator or paper for checking leg × √2 (√2 ≈ 1.414)
- Camera / phone for the portfolio photo

## Part 1 — Fold the Square
1. Measure and record the side length of your square — this is your **leg**.
2. Fold the square exactly corner-to-corner along one diagonal, creasing it flat. This crease splits the square into two congruent 45-45-90 triangles.
3. Unfold and measure the diagonal crease directly with your ruler — this is your **hypotenuse**.
4. Compute leg × √2 and compare it to what you actually measured. How close do they come?

## Part 2 — Find a Second Real 45-45-90 Triangle
5. Measure a real 45-45-90 set-square, or any object with a right-isosceles corner (a mitered frame corner, a cut tile, a folded napkin).
6. Measure both legs (confirm they're congruent) and the hypotenuse, in the same unit.
7. Compute leg × √2 for this object and compare it to the measured hypotenuse.
8. Any gap is almost always the fold or the ruler, not the rule — real-world measuring is never perfectly exact, but hyp = leg√2 is.

## CAD alternative (or extension)
If FreeCAD is set up, sketch a right isosceles triangle with both legs constrained equal, then use the angle tool to confirm both non-right angles land on exactly 45° and measure the hypotenuse against leg × √2. Try stretching one leg longer than the other and watch the 45° angles — and the shortcut — disappear. Export a screenshot for the portfolio.

## Safety
Use scissors or a paper cutter carefully if trimming a square; nothing here requires heat, blades near fingers, or climbing — just folding and measuring.
