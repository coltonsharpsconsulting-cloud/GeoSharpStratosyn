# String-Line Lab

*Make the four building blocks with your hands. When you can string a real ray and walk to where it "keeps going," the difference between a ray and a segment stops being a definition and becomes something you felt.*

**Time:** ~1 hour · **Deliverable:** one board with a point, a segment, a ray, and a line — photographed for your [[Records/Portfolio|Portfolio]].

## Materials
- A piece of foam board, corkboard, or a scrap of plywood (about 30 × 40 cm)
- 6–8 small nails, push-pins, or map tacks
- A ball of bright string or thin yarn
- A ruler or tape measure, a fine marker, a small sticky-note flag
- *(Camera / phone for the portfolio photo)*

## Build it — the four figures

1. **Point.** Push one tack anywhere and label it **P** with a marker. That's it — a point marks a *location*, nothing more. Notice it has no length to measure.
2. **Segment.** Push two tacks, label them **C** and **D**, and run string tightly between them. Both ends stop — this is $\overline{CD}$. **Measure it** with your ruler and write the length on the board.
3. **Ray.** Push one tack **A** near an edge, a second tack **B** a hand-span away, run string from A through B **and keep going off the edge of the board.** Tie a little flag past the edge that says *"forever →"*. This is $\overrightarrow{AB}$ — one endpoint (A), no end the other way.
4. **Line.** Take a fresh pair **E**, **F**, run string through both and let it run off **both** edges, flags on each end: *"↔ forever."* This is $\overleftrightarrow{EF}$.

## Measure & test
- Record the length of segment $\overline{CD}$: **______ cm**.
- Now try to record the length of your **ray** and your **line**. What happens? Write one sentence about *why* you can't.
- Stand your ray up and point along it. Where would B's opposite ray, $\overrightarrow{AB}$'s reverse, go? Add a dotted string to show **opposite rays** from A.

## CAD alternative (or extension)
If FreeCAD is set up, model the same four figures in a Sketch: a **Point**, a **Line** (segment) with a measured length constraint, and use a construction line to represent the "runs forever" idea. Export a screenshot for the portfolio. *(We'll go deeper with CAD as the units build — today just get a point and a measured segment on screen.)*

## Safety
Tacks and nails are sharp — press, don't hammer near your hand; keep points capped when carrying the board.

## Turn in (rubric — 6 pts)
| Criterion | Pts |
|---|---|
| Point **P** labeled; no length claimed for it | 1 |
| Segment **CD** strung *and measured* (length recorded) | 2 |
| Ray **AB** strung with one endpoint + a "forever" flag past one edge | 1 |
| Line **EF** strung with "forever" flags on **both** ends | 1 |
| One clear sentence on why a ray/line can't be measured | 1 |

Photograph the finished board and drop it in the [[Records/Portfolio|Portfolio]]. In Session 3 you'll defend, in words, the difference your hands just made.
