2.A right triangle has legs 3 and 4 and hypotenuse 5. θ sits at the vertex between the leg of 4 and the hypotenuse, so the leg of 3 is opposite θ and the leg of 4 is adjacent to θ. Which ratio equals 4/3?

3.For that same 3-4-5 triangle (opposite = 3, adjacent = 4, hypotenuse = 5), find all six trigonometric ratios for θ. Reduce every fraction.

Answer
sin θ = 3/5; cos θ = 4/5; tan θ = 3/4; csc θ = 5/3; sec θ = 5/4; cot θ = 4/3.

4.Using the baseline (adjacent), height-above-eye (opposite), and hypotenuse you measured and computed in your Build session, recompute sin θ and cos θ as decimals (three places), then confirm sin²θ + cos²θ ≈ 1 using your own real numbers.

Answer
Answers vary by student's own measurements, e.g. sin θ ≈ 0.530, cos θ ≈ 0.848, and 0.530² + 0.848² ≈ 0.281 + 0.719 = 1.000.

5.Explain, from the definitions (not just 'it's the rule'), why csc θ must always equal 1/sin θ.

Answer
sin θ = opp/hyp and csc θ = hyp/opp are the same two side lengths, just with the numerator and denominator swapped. Any fraction times its swapped version equals 1 — (opp/hyp) × (hyp/opp) = (opp×hyp)/(hyp×opp) = 1 — so csc θ is exactly 1 ÷ sin θ.

6.Fill in the blank exactly as on your MUS worksheet: sin²θ + cos²θ = ______. This identity is really the ______ expressed with trigonometric ratios.

Answer
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1. This identity is really the Pythagorean theorem expressed with trigonometric ratios.
