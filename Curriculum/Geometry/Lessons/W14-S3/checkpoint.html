4.A central angle cuts off a minor arc measuring 115°. What is the measure of the major arc, and how do you know?

Answer
245°; minor and major arcs of the same circle always add up to 360°, so 360° − 115° = 245°.

5.On the grid, draw a circle. Mark its center, and label one radius, one diameter, and one sector.

Answer
A circle with its center marked, a segment from the center to the rim labeled as the radius, a segment through the center labeled as the diameter, and a shaded pie-slice region between two radii and the arc connecting them labeled as the sector.

6.In your own words, explain why the diameter must be the longest chord a circle can have.

Answer
Every chord's endpoints connect to the center by two radii, each of length r. If the chord doesn't pass through the center, those two radii and the chord form a triangle, and the triangle inequality says the chord (the third side) must be shorter than the sum of the two radii, 2r. Only when the chord passes straight through the center do the two radii and the chord line up in one straight line — no triangle at all — making the chord exactly 2r. So no non-diameter chord can ever reach 2r; the diameter is always the longest.
