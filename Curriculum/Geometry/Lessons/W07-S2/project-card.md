# Angle-Pair Hunt & X-Stick Build

*Build a real "X" and let a protractor settle the question a definition can only assert: are vertical angles really always equal? Then go hunt a complementary pair and a supplementary pair hiding in your own house. By the end, "vertical angles are congruent" stops being a rule you were told and becomes something your own hands measured.*

**Time:** ~1 hour ¬∑ **Deliverable:** a built and measured X, plus a hunt log of one complementary and one supplementary real-world pair, photographed for your [[Records/Portfolio|Portfolio]].

## Materials
- Two straight sticks, paint stirrers, rulers, or skewers (long enough to see clearly)
- A brass paper fastener, twist-tie, tape, or a pushpin into cardboard ‚Äî anything to pivot the two sticks at one shared point
- A protractor, a fine marker, sticky-note flags for labeling
- Camera / phone for the portfolio photo

## Part 1 ‚Äî Build the X
1. Cross your two sticks near their midpoints (or near one end each, like open scissors) so they pivot freely at one shared point. Push a fastener or pin through both, or tape them so the pivot holds but can still be adjusted.
2. Open the X to any angle you like ‚Äî not 90¬∞, so the four angles aren't all identical. Label the pivot point **H**.
3. Going around H, label the four angles **‚àÝ1, ‚àÝ2, ‚àÝ3, ‚àÝ4** in order, in marker or on sticky flags.

## Part 2 ‚Äî Measure it
4. Set your protractor's center on H and measure each of the four angles to the nearest whole degree. Record all four in order.
5. Circle the two angles that came out (almost) exactly equal ‚Äî those are your **vertical angles**. Underline two angles sitting side by side ‚Äî check that their measures add to about 180¬∞ ‚Äî those are a **linear pair**.

## Part 3 ‚Äî Angle-Pair Hunt
6. Find ONE real **complementary** pair somewhere in your house ‚Äî two angles that together look like they'd fill a square corner. (A folded paper corner cut into two pieces is a reliable one.)
7. Find ONE real **supplementary** pair ‚Äî two angles that together would fill a straight line. (A door propped open against a wall, split by the door's edge, works well.)
8. For each, name the object and record both angles' measured or carefully estimated degrees.

## CAD alternative (or extension)
If FreeCAD is set up, sketch two construction lines crossing at a point and add an angle constraint on one pair. Drag one line and watch the opposite (vertical) angle constraint hold steady while the adjacent ones change ‚Äî the exact behavior you're about to argue for in Session 3. Export a screenshot for the portfolio.

## Safety
Sticks and pins have points ‚Äî press pushpins away from fingers, and keep skewer or stick ends capped or pointed down while carrying the board. If you prop a real door open for the hunt, keep fingers clear of the hinge side.
