# Rationalize Card-Sort & Diagonal-Measure Lab

*Rationalizing a denominator is really a claim about EQUALITY — that 1/√2 and √2/2 are the exact same number, just dressed differently. Today you test that claim with a ruler instead of trusting the algebra alone: build a real diagonal, measure it, and see the two forms land on the same tick mark. Then you drill the mechanics with a rationalizing card sort.*

**Time:** ~1 hour · **Deliverable:** a measurement log (diagonal + half-diagonal + calculator check) and a completed rationalizing card sort, photographed for your [[Records/Portfolio|Portfolio]].

## Materials
- Graph paper (or a printed 1-inch grid)
- A ruler, scissors, a marker, a calculator
- Index cards or slips of paper for the card sort (about 12)
- *(Camera / phone for the portfolio photo)*

## Part 1 — Build & Measure the Diagonal
1. On graph paper, draw a square exactly **1 unit** (or 1 inch) on each side. Draw its diagonal, corner to corner.
2. By the **Pythagorean theorem** (Lesson 18): $1^2 + 1^2 = 2$, so that diagonal is exactly $\sqrt2$ units — an irrational length a ruler can only approximate (about 1.41 units).
3. Measure the diagonal with your ruler as precisely as you can. Record the reading.
4. Mark the diagonal's exact **midpoint** (fold it, or measure and mark with your ruler) and measure that half-length. Record it.

## Part 2 — Calculator Check
5. On a calculator, compute $1 \div$ (your diagonal reading from step 3). Record the decimal.
6. Compare that decimal to your half-diagonal measurement from step 4 — they should be the **same number** (within measuring error). That's $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt2} = \dfrac{\sqrt2}{2}$, confirmed with a ruler instead of algebra: dividing 1 by the whole diagonal gives the same length as taking half of it.

## Part 3 — Rationalizing Card Sort
7. On separate index cards, write six un-rationalized fractions: $\dfrac{5}{\sqrt2}$, $\dfrac{8}{\sqrt5}$, $\dfrac{6}{\sqrt3}$, $\dfrac{9}{\sqrt2}$, $\dfrac{10}{\sqrt5}$, $\dfrac{12}{\sqrt6}$. On six more cards, write their rationalized, simplified forms, shuffled out of order: $\dfrac{9\sqrt2}{2}$, $2\sqrt5$, $2\sqrt6$, $\dfrac{5\sqrt2}{2}$, $\dfrac{8\sqrt5}{5}$, $2\sqrt3$.
8. Shuffle both piles together and match each un-rationalized card to its rationalized-form card. For each match, say out loud which radical you multiplied top and bottom by, and whether the resulting numbers reduced any further.

## CAD alternative (or extension)
If FreeCAD is set up, sketch a 1×1 square, add its diagonal, and use the measurement tool to confirm the diagonal reads √2 (≈1.414) to the tool's precision — a digital version of the ruler check. Export a screenshot for the portfolio.

## Safety
Scissors are sharp — cut away from your fingers, and keep the blade closed when not in use.
