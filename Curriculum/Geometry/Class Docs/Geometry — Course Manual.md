## 2 · Course Description

This is a **full-year, 1-on-1 homeschool Geometry** course for **Stephen Salas**, built on **Math-U-See Geometry** (student workbook, DVD/video instruction, and test booklet). Vault **Lesson N corresponds to Math-U-See Lesson N** throughout — the two number the same lessons.

We meet **three sessions a week**, about an hour each, in person, on a standing rhythm:

- **S1 · Concept** — meet the idea together, connect it to the Math-U-See lesson.
- **S2 · Build** — put it to work: CAD, 3D-printing, real-world measuring, or an art/craft build.
- **S3 · Prove** — checkpoint, present and verify the build, close the loop before the Math-U-See Lesson Test.

The year runs **40 weeks across two 20-week semesters** (roughly August 2026 – May 2027), organized into **three units and 30 lessons**:

- **Unit 01 — Foundations & Plane Figures** (Lessons 1–10)
- **Unit 02 — Area, the Pythagorean Theorem & Similarity** (Lessons 11–19)
- **Unit 03 — Trigonometry, Solids & Coordinate Geometry** (Lessons 20–30)

Each unit closes with a **Math-U-See Unit Test**, and the year closes with a cumulative **Final Exam**. The course is **project-based** throughout — every unit has a hands-on capstone build (CAD and 3D-printing, real-world measuring, art/craft models) that makes the math physical, not just symbolic. On completion, Stephen earns **1.0 Geometry credit** on his homeschool transcript.

> *Everything we build connects back to the lesson it came from — the point is that geometry is something you can hold, not just solve.*

## 4 · How the Week Runs

**Three in-person sessions, about an hour each:**

| # | Session | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | Concept | Meet the idea for the lesson, work through it together, connect it to the Math-U-See material |
| S2 | Build | Hands-on project time — CAD in FreeCAD, 3D-print prep, real-world measuring, or an art/craft build |
| S3 | Prove | Checkpoint problems, present or verify the build, get ready for the Lesson Test |

**Between sessions, independently, Stephen:**

1. Reads the concept note (or watches the Math-U-See instruction) for the lesson.
2. Completes **Lesson Practice A/B** and **Systematic Review C/D** in the workbook.
3. Takes the **Math-U-See Lesson Test** from the test booklet.

The Salas Compass travels between sessions — Stephen reads and logs in it before S1, and the build work gets recorded in it as it happens.

## 5 · Mastery & Progress

This course is **mastery-first**: the goal is that Stephen can actually do the geometry, not that he moved past it on schedule.

Each lesson's mastery record draws on three things:

1. The **Math-U-See Lesson Test**
2. The **session checkpoint** (in S3 · Prove)
3. The **project rubric** for that lesson's build

Together these are recorded as **Emerging**, **Proficient**, or **Mastered**. **Advancement is mastery-gated** — we move to the next lesson once Stephen has genuinely got this one, not just because the calendar says it's time.

The **Math-U-See Unit Tests** (after Lessons 10, 19, and 30) and the cumulative **Final Exam** are the larger checks that confirm mastery is holding across a whole unit — and, at year's end, across the whole course.

---

## 6 · Reassessment

Mastery is **rebuilt, not re-rolled.** If a Lesson Test, Unit Test, or checkpoint comes back below Proficient, the path back is:

1. Redo the related **Lesson Practice / Systematic Review** pages.
2. Correct the original test or checkpoint — find and fix the actual mistake, not just the wrong answer.
3. Retake the assessment.

The mastery record reflects the **highest demonstration**, not an average of attempts — the point of reassessment is to show the standard was eventually met, not to track how many tries it took.

---

## 7 · Assessment Integrity

The **Math-U-See Lesson Tests, Unit Tests, and the Final Exam are completed independently** — no help from Colton, an answer key, or an AI tool while the test is in progress. If Stephen gets stuck mid-test, he stops and flags it for the next session rather than looking it up.

Everything else about honest work — attribution, and where AI fits into the rest of the course — lives in the sibling note **[[Geometry — Academic Integrity & AI]]**.
