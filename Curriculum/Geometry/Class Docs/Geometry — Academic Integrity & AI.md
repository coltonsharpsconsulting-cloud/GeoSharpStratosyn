# Geometry — Academic Integrity & AI

[[Curriculum/Geometry/Class Docs/Geometry — Course Manual|← course manual]]  ·  Stephen Salas · Geometry · homeschool

> *One idea holds this whole note together: be honest about where the work came from — including what came from an AI.*

---

## Honest attribution

If a source helped — a textbook, a website, a video, a person, or an AI tool — say so. Treat an AI tool exactly like any other source: fine to use, fine to name, never fine to pass off as unaided work you didn't actually do.

## The reasoning has to be Stephen's

AI can explain a step, check an answer, or help untangle a concept that isn't clicking — but it can't *be* the thinking. On any work that gets recorded toward mastery, the reasoning that gets to the answer has to be Stephen's own. That reasoning is what's actually being assessed, not just the final number.

## On graded tests, outside help is off

The **Math-U-See Lesson Tests, Unit Tests, and the Final Exam** (see **[[Curriculum/Geometry/Class Docs/Geometry — Course Manual|Course Manual]]**, §7) are completed **independently** — no AI, no outside help, no exceptions. Everywhere else in the course — reading the concept, working the Build session, doing Lesson Practice and Systematic Review — AI is welcome as a tutor: ask it to explain a step differently, check reasoning, or explore an idea further.

## Cite it like any source

Used AI to understand or check something? Say so, briefly — in the Salas Compass, on a build write-up, or in conversation. Properly cited AI use is just... use. Uncited AI work passed off as unaided is the one thing this note asks Stephen never to do.
